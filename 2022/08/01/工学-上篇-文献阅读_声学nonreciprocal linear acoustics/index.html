Broadband nonreciprocal linear acoustics through a non-local active metamaterial


Abstract

The ability to create linear systems that manifest broadband nonreciprocal wave propagation would provide forexquisite control over acoustic signals for electronic filtering in communication and noise control.
Broadband nonreciprocal wave propagation：声波宽带单向传播

Acoustic nonreciprocity has predominately been achieved by approaches that introduce nonlinear interaction, mean-flow biasing, smart skins, and spatio-temporal parametric modulation into the system. Each approach suffers from at least one of the following drawbacks: the introduction of modulation tones, narrow band filtering, and the interruption of mean flow in fluid acoustics.
Drawbacks of previous approaches

We now show that an acoustic media that is non-local and active provides a new means to break reciprocity in a linear fashion without these deleterious effects. We realize this media using a distributed network of interlaced subwavelength sensor–actuator pairs with unidirectional signal transport. We exploit this new design space to create a stable metamaterial with non-even dispersion relations and electronically tunable nonreciprocal behavior over a broad range of frequencies.

nonreciprocity 非互易性 ：非互易性是微波技术和材料学科中的用语，指电磁波在某物体中沿相反的两个方向传输会呈现不同的电磁损耗、相移等特性，把这一种现象称为非互易性。

Introduction

Reciprocity → Nonreciprocity

Approaches:

  • Biasing external magnetic field
  • Mean flow in the acoustic medium
  • Biasing DC electric field in 压电半导体
  • DC magnetic field in nagnetoelastic and polar media
  • Break spatial or temporal symmetry in the governing equations
    • Introduce nonlinear interactions
    • Spatiotemporal modulation of the properties of the medium
    • Need later demodulation or signal processing to prevent signal corruption
  • Sensor-actuator pairs to modulate the wave propagation in the medium in a linear fashion

Conclusions

  1. This technique can be adopted to many diff. wave-bearing media and systems, e.g. waveguid system, bulk-wave piezoelectric elements. using the feed-forward control of the NAM to create nonreciprocity.
  2. Can use collocated sensor-actuator patch approaches to control wave propagation on beams, converting to nonreciprocal systems by f-f control.
  3. Envision creating nonreciprocal systems in 2D media.
  4. Flexibility in designing systems with desired IF magnitudes & frequencies.
  5. Tunability accomplished with a controller consisting of a spatially constant gain with simple spectural variations.
  6. Potential for noise control and for enhancing the performance of electromechanical filters and amplifiers.
